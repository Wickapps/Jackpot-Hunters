## Penn Entertainment (PENN) -- Most Detailed Disclosures

Penn is the only operator that reports a **specific "Accrued Progressive Jackpot Liability"** as a separate current liability line item, with its own custom XBRL tag (`penn_AccruedProgressiveJackpotLiabilityCurrent`).

### Progressive Jackpot Liability Trend

| Period End    | Jackpot Liability | YoY Change |
|---------------|------------------:|-----------:|
| Dec 31, 2021  |         $47.6M    |     --     |
| Dec 31, 2022  |         $51.4M    |    +8.0%   |
| Dec 31, 2023  |         $60.8M    |   +18.3%   |
| Dec 31, 2024  |         $69.3M    |   +14.0%   |
| Dec 31, 2025  |         $79.1M    |   +14.1%   |

The liability has grown **66% over four years**, from $47.6M to $79.1M, reflecting expanding progressive jackpot pools across Penn's property portfolio and online gaming operations.

### Other Notable Disclosures

- **Large Payout Risk:** Penn discloses that "certain VIP patrons have placed bets or series of bets that resulted in large payouts and negatively impacted our results of operations."
- **Slot Revenue Growth:** Q3 2025 revenues increased $37.2M vs. prior year, primarily from online slot revenue growth at the Interactive segment. Northeast segment saw $8.3M increase driven by slot revenues.
- **Deferred Compensation:** Reported alongside jackpot liability -- $107.2M (2024) and $119.3M (2025).

## Caesars Entertainment (CZR) -- Moderate Disclosures

Caesars mentions progressive jackpots in accounting policy boilerplate but does **not** break out a specific jackpot liability line item.

### Hold Percentages

| Metric                         | Typical Range     |
|--------------------------------|-------------------|
| Slot win % (of handle)         | 9% -- 11%         |
| Table game hold % (of drop)    | 16% -- 23%        |
| Sports betting hold            | 7% -- 11%         |
| iGaming hold                   | ~3%               |

### Other Notes

- Slot revenues generate the **majority of casino revenues** (~55--58% of total net revenues across reporting periods).
- Sports betting and iGaming hold percentages consistently reported as "within our typical range."
- Caesars flags that "variations in hold percentages and increases in promotional and marketing expenses in highly competitive markets may negatively impact Caesars Digital's net revenues."

## MGM Resorts International (MGM) -- Minimal Jackpot Disclosures

MGM's filings contain **no progressive jackpot-specific disclosures**. Matches are almost entirely standard "gaming wins and losses" accounting language and hold percentage metrics.

### Hold Percentages (Las Vegas Strip Resorts)

| Metric                               | Typical Range     |
|--------------------------------------|-------------------|
| Table game hold -- Baccarat (of drop) | 25% -- 35%       |
| Table game hold -- Non-Baccarat       | 19% -- 23%       |

### Key Language

> "The transaction price for a casino wager is the difference between gaming wins and losses ('net win'). Discounts on markers and commissions rebated to players are recorded as a reduction of casino revenue."

### Other Notes

- MGM does not separately disclose slot win percentages or progressive jackpot accruals in the same detail as Penn or Caesars.
- Recent filings (2025--2026) shifted language to describe "Win" or "hold" percentages as "the net amount of gaming wins and losses in relation to table games drop or slot handle."
- One 10-Q referenced Japan gaming tax treatment (MGM Osaka), noting individual gaming wins would not require income tax reporting.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Penn Entertainment is the gold standard** for progressive jackpot disclosure among these three operators. It is the only one reporting a dedicated balance sheet line item with dollar amounts that can be tracked over time.

2. **The progressive jackpot liability is growing steadily** at Penn, suggesting expanding jackpot pools and/or more progressive machines in operation. The $79.1M figure as of Dec 2025 is a significant current liability.

3. **Caesars provides policy-level disclosure** but lumps jackpot accruals into broader liability categories, making it difficult to isolate the jackpot-specific amount.

4. **MGM provides the least jackpot-specific information**, focusing instead on hold percentages and standard revenue recognition language. Their baccarat-heavy Las Vegas Strip business has different risk characteristics than the regional/online-focused PENN portfolio.

5. **Online gaming metrics are emerging** -- Penn's recent filings now include detailed online slot, table game, and sportsbook hold percentages, reflecting the growing importance of the Interactive segment.
